# Databricks

**Databricks, Inc.** is an American enterprise software company headquartered in [[San Francisco]], California, specializing in [[data analytics]], [[data engineering]], and [[artificial intelligence]]. Founded in 2013 by the original creators of [[Apache Spark]], the company pioneered the "[[data lakehouse]]" architecture—a unified approach combining elements of [[data warehouse]]s and [[data lake]]s.

As of December 2025, Databricks was valued at $134 billion following a $4 billion Series L funding round, making it one of the most valuable private technology companies in the world.

## History

### Origins and Founding

Databricks emerged from the [[AMPLab]] project at the [[University of California, Berkeley]], where researchers developed Apache Spark, an open-source distributed computing framework. The company was founded in 2013 by seven co-founders: [[Ali Ghodsi]], [[Andy Konwinski]], Arsalan Tavakoli-Shiraji, [[Ion Stoica]], [[Matei Zaharia]], Patrick Wendell, and [[Reynold Xin]].

### Early Growth (2013–2020)

Databricks secured initial funding from [[Andreessen Horowitz]] in 2013, raising $13.9 million in its Series A round. The company positioned itself as an alternative to [[Google]]'s [[MapReduce]] system. In 2017, [[Microsoft Azure]] integrated Databricks as Azure Databricks, marking a significant enterprise partnership.

### Rapid Expansion (2021–2024)

The company's growth accelerated dramatically:

- **February 2021**: Raised $1 billion at a $28 billion valuation, with investors including [[Amazon Web Services]], [[CapitalG]], and [[Salesforce Ventures]]. Reported over 5,000 customers.
- **February 2021**: Launched integration with [[Google Cloud]], [[Kubernetes]] Engine, and [[BigQuery]]
- **August 2021**: Completed $1.6 billion Series H at $38 billion valuation
- **September 2023**: Raised $500 million at $43 billion valuation, with [[NVIDIA]] and Capital One Ventures participating
- **December 2024**: Announced landmark $10 billion funding round at $62 billion valuation

### 2025 Developments

Databricks continued its aggressive expansion in 2025:

- **March 2025**: Announced $1 billion investment in San Francisco's downtown
- **March 2025**: Entered five-year, $100 million partnership with [[Anthropic]] to incorporate Claude into the Databricks platform
- **April 2025**: Featured in [[Forbes]] AI 50 list
- **June 2025**: Signed four-year partnership with [[Alphabet Inc.|Alphabet]] to integrate [[Gemini (language model)|Gemini]]; launched Agent Bricks and Lakebase
- **August 2025**: Raised $1 billion Series K at $100+ billion valuation
- **September 2025**: Partnered with [[OpenAI]] in a $100 million deal to incorporate OpenAI's LLMs
- **December 2025**: Completed $4 billion Series L at $134 billion valuation

## Products and Services

### Data Intelligence Platform

Databricks' flagship offering is its cloud-based Data Intelligence Platform, which combines the company's lakehouse architecture with [[generative AI]] capabilities. The platform integrates data engineering, data science, [[machine learning]], and business intelligence on a unified system.

Key platform components:

- **Databricks SQL**: Business intelligence and analytics reporting on data lakes, compatible with tools like [[Tableau]], Looker, and ThoughtSpot. Projected to reach $1 billion revenue run rate by 2025.
- **Delta Lake**: Open-source project adding [[ACID]] transaction support to data lakes
- **MLflow**: Open-source platform for managing machine learning workflows

### Artificial Intelligence Products

#### DBRX

Released in March 2024, [[DBRX]] is Databricks' open-source foundation model built on a [[mixture-of-experts]] architecture. Key specifications:
- 136 billion total parameters (36 billion active per output)
- Cost approximately $10 million to develop
- Released under the Databricks Open Model License
- Outperformed [[Llama 2]] on logic puzzles and general knowledge benchmarks

#### Mosaic AI Suite

Launched in early 2024, the Mosaic tools (acquired from [[MosaicML]]) enable:
- **AI Vector Search**: Building [[retrieval-augmented generation]] (RAG) models
- **AI Model Serving**: Deploying, governing, and monitoring fine-tuned models
- **AI Pretraining**: Platform for enterprises to create custom LLMs

#### Agent Bricks

Announced at the 2025 Data + AI Summit, Agent Bricks is a development platform for building [[AI agent]]s.

#### Databricks One

A no-code AI business intelligence platform introduced in 2025.

### Dolly

In March 2023, Databricks released Dolly, an open-source [[language model]] with 6 billion parameters that developers can use to create custom [[chatbot]]s. Named after [[Dolly the sheep]], the company claimed it demonstrated "ChatGPT-like instruction following ability."

### Lakebase

Launched June 2025, Lakebase is an [[OLTP]] transactional database designed to complement the lakehouse architecture.
