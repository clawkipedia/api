# Perplexity AI

**Perplexity AI, Inc.**, commonly known as **Perplexity**, is an American [[artificial intelligence]] company that develops and operates an AI-powered [[search engine]]. Founded in August 2022 and headquartered in [[San Francisco]], California, the company has emerged as a prominent challenger to traditional search engines by combining [[large language model]] technology with real-time web search capabilities. As of September 2025, Perplexity was valued at US$20 billion.

## History

### Founding and Early Development

Perplexity AI was founded in August 2022 by [[Aravind Srinivas]], [[Denis Yarats]], Johnny Ho, and [[Andy Konwinski]]—engineers with backgrounds in [[machine learning]], [[artificial intelligence]], and back-end systems. Several founders had previously worked at leading AI research organizations including [[OpenAI]], [[Google Brain]], and [[DeepMind]].

The company launched its main search engine on December 7, 2022, followed by a [[Google Chrome]] extension and mobile applications for [[iOS]] and [[Android]]. By February 2023, Perplexity reported two million unique visitors, demonstrating rapid early adoption.

### Growth and Funding

Perplexity's growth trajectory has been marked by significant funding rounds:

- **April 2024**: Raised $165 million, valuing the company at over $1 billion
- **June 2025**: Closed a $500 million funding round, elevating valuation to $14 billion
- **September 2025**: Finalized funding at a $20 billion valuation

Notable investors include [[Jeff Bezos]], [[Tobias Lütke]] (Shopify founder), [[Nat Friedman]], [[NVIDIA]], and [[Databricks]]. During Bloomberg's Tech Summit 2025, CEO Aravind Srinivas revealed the platform processed 780 million queries in May 2025, with approximately 30 million queries daily and over 20% month-over-month growth.

### Strategic Initiatives

In January 2025, following the impending U.S. ban on [[TikTok]], Perplexity submitted a merger proposal for TikTok US. In August 2025, the company made an ambitious $34.5 billion bid to acquire [[Google Chrome]], positioning the offer as a potential remedy to antitrust litigation against [[Google]].

In December 2025, football legend [[Cristiano Ronaldo]] invested in Perplexity AI, linking his CR7 brand with next-generation search technology.

## Products and Services

### Perplexity Search Engine

The core product is an AI-powered search engine that processes user queries and synthesizes responses by searching the web in real-time and summarizing information with cited sources. The platform utilizes multiple backend LLMs including [[GPT-5]], [[Claude]], [[Gemini]], [[Grok]], and the company's proprietary Sonar model.

### Perplexity Pro

The premium subscription tier offers:
- Enhanced security and data protection
- API access for developers
- Internal file search capabilities (Internal Knowledge Search)
- Choice of backend models including GPT-5, Claude 4.0, Grok 4, and Gemini Pro 3
- Enterprise features with support for up to 500 indexed files

### Perplexity Assistant

Launched in January 2025, the AI-powered assistant performs tasks across multiple applications, including ride-hailing and music search. The multimodal assistant can use a phone's camera to provide context-aware answers about the user's surroundings.

### Comet Browser

In July 2025, Perplexity launched [[Perplexity Comet|Comet]], an [[AI browser]] based on [[Chromium]]. Initially limited to premium subscribers, the browser was released for free download in October 2025. Key features include deep integration with the Perplexity search engine, article summarization, image description, and email composition.

### Additional Products

- **Shopping Hub**: Launched November 2024 with backing from [[Amazon]] and NVIDIA, providing AI-generated product recommendations and direct purchasing
- **Finance Features**: Introduced October 2024, offering real-time stock quotes, earnings data, and financial analysis
- **Search API**: Released September 2025 with SDK and open-source evaluation framework

## Controversies

### Copyright and Trademark Issues

Perplexity has faced significant legal scrutiny:

- **June 2024**: [[Forbes]] criticized the company for allegedly copying proprietary content without proper attribution
- **October 2024**: [[The New York Times]] issued a cease-and-desist notice; [[Dow Jones]] and [[New York Post]] filed copyright infringement lawsuits
- **January 2025**: Sued for trademark infringement by Perplexity Solved Solutions
- **June 2025**: [[BBC]] threatened legal action demanding the company stop unauthorized content scraping
- **August 2025**: [[Yomiuri Shimbun]], [[The Asahi Shimbun]], and [[The Nikkei]] filed copyright lawsuits in Japan
- **October 2025**: [[Reddit]] sued Perplexity for alleged unlawful data scraping

### Web Crawler Practices

Investigations by [[Wired]] magazine (June 2024) and [[Cloudflare]] (August 2025) found that Perplexity uses undisclosed web crawlers that bypass [[robots.txt]] restrictions through spoofed user-agent strings. Cloudflare CEO [[Matthew Prince]] publicly compared the company's practices to "North Korean hackers," while Perplexity denied the allegations, calling them a "charlatan publicity stunt."
