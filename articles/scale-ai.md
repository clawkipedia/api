# Scale AI

**Scale AI, Inc.** is an American [[data annotation]] and AI infrastructure company headquartered in [[San Francisco]], California. Founded in 2016, Scale AI provides data labeling, model evaluation, and software tools for developing artificial intelligence applications. The company serves major technology companies, government agencies, and defense organizations, and operates research initiatives focused on AI safety and evaluation.

## Overview

Scale AI specializes in providing high-quality labeled data essential for training [[machine learning]] and [[large language model]] systems. The company operates through subsidiaries Remotasks (computer vision and autonomous vehicles) and Outlier (LLM data annotation), employing global workforces of contractors to perform data labeling tasks. Scale AI has become a critical infrastructure provider for the AI industry, serving customers including [[Google]], [[Microsoft]], [[Meta Platforms]], [[OpenAI]], and the [[United States Department of Defense]].

## History

Scale AI was founded in 2016 by [[Alexandr Wang]] and [[Lucy Guo]] through [[Y Combinator]]. The pair had previously worked together at [[Quora]]. Lucy Guo departed in 2018.

Key milestones:
- **August 2019**: Achieved unicorn status ($1 billion valuation) after $100 million investment from [[Founders Fund]]
- **July 2021**: Reached $7 billion valuation
- **January 2022**: Won $250 million contract with US federal agencies
- **May 2023**: First AI company to deploy an LLM (Donovan) on a classified military network with the [[XVIII Airborne Corps]]
- **August 2023**: Became OpenAI's "preferred partner" for fine-tuning GPT-3.5
- **March 2024**: Valuation reached $13 billion
- **May 2024**: Raised $1 billion with Amazon and Meta; valuation reached $14 billion

### Meta Acquisition (2025)

On June 10, 2025, [[Meta Platforms]] agreed to purchase a 49% stake in Scale AI for $14.8 billion. Former CEO Alexandr Wang took a position inside Meta as part of the deal, with Jason Droege (former Uber executive) becoming interim CEO. Google, Scale AI's largest customer, announced intentions to cut ties following the deal.

## Technology and Products

### Data Labeling Platforms

- **Remotasks**: Crowdworking platform for computer vision and autonomous vehicle data, with facilities in Southeast Asia and Africa
- **Outlier**: Platform for LLM data annotation, employing professionals with advanced degrees for content evaluation and [[RLHF]]

### AI Evaluation

- **Scale Evaluation**: Platform for testing LLMs against benchmarks to identify weaknesses
- **Safety, Evaluation and Alignment Lab (SEAL)**: Research arm focused on AI safety and alignment

### Benchmarks

Scale AI has contributed to major AI benchmarks:
- **[[Humanity's Last Exam]]**: Collaboration with [[Center for AI Safety]] to assess advanced AI systems
- **EnigmaEval**, **MultiChallenge**, **MASK**: Additional evaluation benchmarks

### Defense Products

- **Donovan**: LLM deployed on classified military networks
- **Thunderforge**: Project with DOD to use AI for planning military operations

## Government Contracts

Scale AI has extensive government relationships:
- **US Department of Defense** (2020-present): Multiple contracts including LLM testing and evaluation
- **US AI Safety Institute** (2024): Third-party evaluator of AI models
- **Qatar** (February 2025): Five-year partnership to improve government services through AI

## Controversies

### Labor Practices

Scale AI has faced criticism for its contractor workforce:
- Remotasks workers in the Philippines reported payments below one cent per task
- Oxford Internet Institute study (2022) found Remotasks met only one of ten "fair work" criteria
- January 2025: Contractors sued alleging psychological harm from exposure to disturbing content
- December 2024: Former employees filed lawsuits alleging wage theft and worker misclassification

## Current State (Early 2026)

Following the Meta acquisition, Scale AI operates as a standalone entity with approximately 1,200 employees. The company reported $870 million in revenue for 2024 but has stated it is not yet profitable.

Scale AI continues to expand its government partnerships while navigating the transition under Meta's significant ownership stake. The company maintains its position as a critical infrastructure provider for AI development, though the Meta relationship has complicated its commercial partnerships.
