# Microsoft Research AI

**Microsoft Research** (MSR) is the research subsidiary of [[Microsoft]], conducting fundamental and applied research in [[artificial intelligence]] and numerous other computing domains. Founded in 1991 by [[Richard Rashid]], [[Bill Gates]], and [[Nathan Myhrvold]], Microsoft Research has become one of the world's largest and most influential corporate research organizations, with significant contributions to AI technology that powers products from [[Bing]] to [[Cortana]] to [[Microsoft Copilot]].

## Overview

Microsoft Research employs over 1,000 computer scientists, physicists, engineers, and mathematicians across research laboratories worldwide. The organization's staff includes [[Turing Award]] winners, [[Fields Medal]] winners, [[MacArthur Fellows]], and [[Dijkstra Prize]] winners.

Between 2010 and 2018, Microsoft held the largest percentage (20%) of the 154,000 AI patents filed worldwide. The company has invested approximately $6 billion annually in research from 2002 to 2010, increasing to $10-14 billion annually since 2010.

The current corporate vice president overseeing Microsoft Research is [[Lili Cheng]].

## Research Structure

Microsoft Research is organized into several divisions:

### Core Research Labs

Microsoft Research includes the core MSR labs along with specialized divisions:

- **Microsoft Research**: Fundamental computer science research
- **Microsoft Research AI**: Focused AI research initiatives
- **Microsoft Research NExT** (New Experiences and Technologies): Applied research and incubation
- **Various incubation efforts**: Experimental projects and prototypes

## Research Laboratories

### Active Laboratories

- **Microsoft Research Redmond** (1991): Founded on the Microsoft campus with approximately 350 researchers. Focuses on theory, AI, machine learning, systems, networking, security, privacy, and human-computer interaction. Headed by Donald Kossmann.

- **Microsoft Research Cambridge, UK** (1997): Founded by [[Roger Needham]], headed by [[Christopher Bishop]]. Focuses on machine learning, security, and information retrieval. Maintains close ties to the [[University of Cambridge]].

- **Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA)** (1998): Located in Beijing with over 300 researchers plus 300 visiting scientists and students. Focuses on natural user interfaces, multimedia, data-intensive computing, search, natural language processing, and computer science fundamentals. Has satellite offices in Shanghai.

- **Microsoft Research India** (Bangalore): Headed by Sriram Rajamani.

- **Microsoft Research Station Q** (2006): Located at [[UC Santa Barbara]], focused on [[quantum computing]] research led by [[Michael Freedman]]. The Quantum Architectures and Computation (QuARC) group is based in Redmond, with satellite locations at Delft, Purdue, Copenhagen, and Sydney.

- **Microsoft Research New England** (2008): Located in Cambridge, Massachusetts near MIT. Founded by [[Jennifer Chayes]], now managed by [[Susan Dumais]]. Focuses on interdisciplinary research combining computer science and social science.

- **Microsoft Research New York City** (2012): Also managed by Susan Dumais. Focuses on computational social sciences, computational economics, machine learning, and information retrieval.

- **Microsoft Research Montreal** (2017): Established after Microsoft's acquisition of [[Maluuba]]. Focuses on natural language processing, machine reading comprehension, deep learning, and reinforcement learning.

- **Gray Systems Lab** (2008): Located in Madison, Wisconsin. Named after [[Jim Gray]], focuses on database technologies.

- **Microsoft Research Asia - Tokyo** (November 2024): Headed by Yasuyuki Matsushita. Focuses on embodied AI, societal AI, well-being, neuroscience, and industry innovation.

### Former Laboratories

- **Microsoft Research Silicon Valley** (2001-2014): Located in Mountain View, focused on distributed computing, security, protocols, fault-tolerance, and large-scale systems before closing in September 2014.

- **[[Microsoft Translator]]**: Neural machine translation
- **[[LinkedIn]]**: AI-powered recommendations and features
- **[[Havok]]**: Physics simulation with AI integration
- **[[Microsoft Dynamics]]**: Business applications with AI capabilities

## Microsoft AI Division

Distinct from Microsoft Research, Microsoft established **Microsoft AI** as a consumer-focused AI unit in March 2024, led by [[Mustafa Suleyman]] (co-founder of [[DeepMind]]) as EVP and CEO. This division focuses on consumer AI products including Copilot.
